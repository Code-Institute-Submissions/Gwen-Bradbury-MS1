DID YOU KNOW? orange aeroplane

On March 8th 1910, Raymonde de Laroche became the world's first licenced female pilot.
Yet in 2020, only a mere 5.26% of pilots are women. And of all captains, only 1.42% are female.
According to statistics, from the International Society of Women Airline Pilots, the total number of women captains in europe, wouldn't even fill a Boeing 747.

Because of this, many organisations have been created to promote women in aviation and get more ladies into the captains seat! Offering encouragement, support and unity, in the aviation industry, these organisations have become a place of belonging for many women across the globe.
One of these organisations being 'The 99's, International Organization of Women Pilots'.

It's not all about commercial aviation either!
Many pilots fly just for fun!
Known as general aviation, or GA for short, this popular type of flying can involve nothing more than jumping in, and going for a fly! Or maybe, with a bit more planning, even landing away at another airport!

And why not try aerobatics, if you fancy something a little more extreme!

What ever your preferance, though, the world of aviation will definitely not disappoint!
But it definitely needs more women!
